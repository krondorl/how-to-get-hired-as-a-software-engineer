## Soft Skills

In the last 4 years the importance of soft skills got stronger.

In 2026 and beyond a person cannot get a job without good soft skills. Hard skills are not enough.

You will need the following soft skills:

- interviewing skills,
- communication,
- problem solving,
- critical thinking,
- creativity,
- collaboration,
- adapting to different people types,
- system thinking.

## Portfolio

Generally hiring managers and even developers rarely check your portfolio. They are looking for company based work experience.

However sometimes it can be useful to have some projects to show.

Projects are useful for personal development or as a coding journal.

If you want to show a project to a company, make sure

- it is related to the given business domain (eg. for a fintech company, show a fintech project)
- it has documentation
- it has unit tests
- it can be scaled
- it has architecture diagrams.

## AI Usage

This means:

- Computer Science
- Data Structures and Algorithms

## CV Writing

- The hiring manager will take 5–10 seconds to look at your CV, so it is important that it is simple and easy to scan.
- Stick to 1–2 pages maximum. You only need to write about the last 5–10 years.
- Create 2–3 master CVs and spend 5 minutes to tailor the CV to the job posting (order keywords, use normal and bold text, delete what is not relevant).

[Use Gergely Orosz’s template for making a CV.](https://blog.pragmaticengineer.com/the-pragmatic-engineers-resume-template/)

## Targeting the Hidden Job Market

This means that you try to find jobs that are not posted anywhere.

For this, you need to do networking and company research. You can do this online (preferred if you are introverted) or in person (meetups, workshops, conferences).

Example Cold Approach Letter

> Dear X.Y.,
>
> I would like to express my interest in full-stack opportunities, including positions that may not be currently advertised.
>
> I am available on short notice for a new project or position.
>
> Briefly about me:
>
> - 5+ years of full-stack experience
> - frontend: Angular, React, TypeScript
> - backend: Java, Node.js
> - databases: PostgreSQL, MongoDB, Oracle, MySQL
>
> I would be happy to discuss opportunities, even on short notice, with immediate availability.
>
> Best regards, John Doe

## Networking

- You can have conversations with acquaintances and strangers as well.
- If you have a good LinkedIn presence with good photos and a well-written profile, start friending people and send simple messages. Ask about their current stack, ask about their technical challenges, and have a conversation.
- You can also mention that you are searching for a job.
- It is worth using Discord and Slack chatrooms as well. Discord chat rooms often have #job and #career rooms.

Example Cold Approach Letter

> Hi X.Y.,
>
> Thanks for accepting my connection request.
>
> I came across your company as a software engineer and I'm really interested learning more about it.
>
> Which tech stack is used at the company?
>
> Best regards, John Doe

## Follow-Up

When applying for jobs, try to research

- the LinkedIn contact,
- email addresses,
- and telephone numbers.

Use either or all of these and do follow-ups

- every 2–3 days
- or at least weekly.

Example search for company recruiters

> Open [perplexity](https://www.perplexity.ai/)
>
> PROMPT: list the recruiters LinkedIn profiles at Company
>
> PROMPT: list the recruiters email addresses at Company
>
> PROMPT: list the telephone numbers at Company

Example follow up letter

> Dear Hiring Manager Name,
>
> I wanted to follow up on my application for the Full Stack Software Engineer position and express my continued interest in the role. With my experience in frontend and backend development, I believe I could contribute effectively to your engineering team.
>
> Thank you for your time and consideration.

## Remote Jobs

Unfortunately, since 2022, remote jobs have become

- extremely competitive and
- demanding
- with lower pay.

You can still get hired, but it is much easier to target a local big city in your country or region and do a hybrid job in terms of competition.

## Hybrid Jobs

I would recommend hybrid jobs (that means both onsite and home office), because there is less competition compared to remote ones.

Also, internships and junior jobs are usually not possible, but in a hybrid setting, they are.

## Avoiding Ghost Jobs

Ghost jobs are fake or no‑longer‑active postings that sit on LinkedIn and Indeed to collect resumes, test the market, or meet hiring‑pipeline quotas. Here’s how to filter them out while still using these platforms productively.

Ghost Job Signals:

- too many applicants
- "30+ days ago" posting date
- "easy apply" badge
- no application confirmation
- no automated email confirmation
- low company response rate

## Real Success Stories

The situation and the job market are difficult, but there is still room for success.

I anonymized all the people in these cases.

- John, a Senior Backend Engineer from Europe (8+ years of work experience), found a job in **6 weeks** in 2026.
- Axel, a Senior Frontend and Full Stack Engineer from Europe (10+ years of work experience), found a job in **4 weeks** in 2026.
- Brad, a Junior Automation Test Engineer (3 years of prep, **0 work experience**) in Europe, found his first job in around **4–6 months** in 2025.

**Warning:** years of work experience and seniority doesn't guarantee that you will be hired. These are only proofs that a person can get a job in weeks or a couple of months and not 12 months.

**PS:** I have seen senior engineers with 10–20 years of experience struggling to get a job because of few interview experience.
